## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into sending money or sharing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud usually targets students and individuals who frequently use digital payment apps.

In a typical scenario, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. They create urgency by saying there is a problem with the user’s account or that money needs to be received. The victim is then asked to click on a malicious link or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app.

Step-by-step, the victim receives a fake request, enters their UPI PIN thinking they are receiving money, and unknowingly authorizes a payment. In some cases, attackers also ask for OTPs or screen-sharing access.

The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, unauthorized transactions, and compromise of personal data. Students are especially vulnerable due to lack of awareness and frequent online transactions.

This case highlights the importance of understanding how digital payments work and being cautious while handling unknown requests online.
